<h2 style="margin: 60px 0px 10px;">About Me</h2>

<div class="justify-text">
I am a Ph.D. Candidate in the <a href="https://ece.gatech.edu/">School of Electrical and Computer Engineering</a> at the <a href="https://www.gatech.edu/">Georgia Institute of Technology</a> in Atlanta, advised by <a href="https://greenlab.ece.gatech.edu/">Dr. Saibal Mukhopadhyay</a>. My research lies at the intersection of <strong>multimodal fusion</strong>, <strong>efficient perception for the edge</strong>, and <strong>state-space models</strong> — I develop novel architectures and algorithms that bridge the gap between high-performance machine learning and real-world deployment on resource-constrained platforms.
</div>

<div class="justify-text">
My work spans <strong>large language models</strong>, <strong>efficient machine learning</strong>, and <strong>quantum sensing</strong>, where I build simulation frameworks and design tools for quantum photonic sensors targeting low-power, high-precision applications. My research has been featured in top-tier venues including <a href="https://cvpr.thecvf.com/">CVPR</a>, <a href="https://wacv.thecvf.com/">WACV</a>, <a href="https://www.ijcnn.org/">IJCNN</a>, <a href="https://www.igarss.org/">IGARSS</a>, <a href="https://qip2024.tw/">QIP</a>, <strong>IEEE Transactions on Radar Systems</strong>, and <a href="https://www.springer.com/series/7092">Studies in Computational Intelligence</a>.
</div>

<div class="justify-text" style="margin-top: 1em;">
<strong style="color:#e74d3c; font-weight:600">Looking ahead, my research will focus on multimodal radar-vision fusion, efficient neural architectures for edge deployment, and hardware-classical co-design for photonic quantum systems.</strong>
</div>

</div>
