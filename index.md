<h2 style="margin: 60px 0px 10px;">About Me</h2>

<div class="justify-text">
I am a Ph.D. Candidate in the <a href="https://ece.gatech.edu/">School of Electrical and Computer Engineering</a> at the <a href="https://www.gatech.edu/">Georgia Institute of Technology</a> in Atlanta, advised by <a href="https://greenlab.ece.gatech.edu/">Dr. Saibal Mukhopadhyay</a>. My research focuses on building <strong>efficient perception systems for autonomous systems</strong> that operate reliably in complex, real-world environments. I develop <strong>lightweight multimodal architectures</strong> that enable robust object detection, segmentation, and scene understanding while remaining deployable on resource-constrained edge platforms.
</div>

<div class="justify-text">
My work spans <strong>multimodal learning/strong> for autonomous perception, <strong>state-space models</strong> for efficient sequential processing, and <strong>HW-SW co-design of neural architectures</strong> for real-time edge inference. I am also interested in <strong>large language models</strong>, <strong>generative modeling</strong>, <strong>efficient computer vision</strong>, <strong>learning from unstructured data</strong>, and <strong>quantum sensing</strong>, where I build simulation frameworks for quantum photonic sensors targeting low-power, high-precision applications. My research has been featured in top-tier venues including <a href="https://cvpr.thecvf.com/">CVPR</a>, <a href="https://wacv.thecvf.com/">WACV</a>, <a href="https://www.ijcnn.org/">IJCNN</a>, <a href="https://www.igarss.org/">IGARSS</a>, <a href="https://qip2024.tw/">QIP</a>, <strong>IEEE Transactions on Radar Systems</strong>, and <a href="https://www.springer.com/series/7092">Studies in Computational Intelligence</a>.
</div>

<div class="justify-text" style="margin-top: 1em;">
<strong style="color:#e74d3c; font-weight:600">Looking ahead, my research will focus on scalable multimodal perception for autonomous systems, efficient neural architectures for real-time edge deployment, and co-design methodologies that unify hardware and software for next-generation intelligent sensing.</strong>
</div>

</div>
